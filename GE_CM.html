Clay Minerals

Elements (atoms) combine to form structural units, that together form unit cells (the smallest subdivision of a crystal), which grouped create minerals.

Most common elements in soil:

  • Oxygen \(O^{-2}\)
  • Silicon \(Si^{+4}\)
  • Hydrogen \(H^{+1}\)
  • Nitrogen \(Ni^{+3}\)
  • Aluminum \(Al^{+3}\)
  • Magnesium \(Mg^{+2}\)

Structural Units

  • Silica Tetrahedron* \((SiO_4)^{4-}\)
    • Negatively charged \(\therefore\) needs to combine to satisfy neutrality;
    • Highly charged and well packed;
    • Quartz is an electrically neutral combination of silica tetrahedron \((SiO_4)\).
  • Alumina Octahedron
    • Gibbsite \(Al_2(OH)_6 \to [Al_{2/3}(OH)_6]^{4-}\);
    • Brucite \(Mg_3(OH)_6 \to [Mg(OH)_6]^{4-}\).

Silicates (Mineral)

  • Combinations of silica tetrahedron;
  • The structural units only share one oxygen;
  • Combinations (in order of drecreasing stability):
    • Island;
    • Double island;
    • Ring;
    • Chain;
    • Double chain;
    • Sheets;
    • Frameworks.

Isomorphous Substitution

  • In order to form the clay, the crystals need to undergo isomorphous substitution, which is the replacement of ions of one type by ions of another type, with the same or different valance, but with retention of the same crystal structure (from the greak: iso = same, morphous = form or shape);
  • The new ions may have different valance, therefore negative charges accumulates;
  • Happens during geological time.

Cation Exchange Capacity

  • Is the charge required to neutralize the electronegativity of a given mass of dry clay.
    • Avogadro’s Number: \(N_A = 6.02214 \times 10^{23}\, mol^{-1}\)
    • Atomic Weight: the weight of one mol of an atom or molecule, \(W_a\)
    • Valance: Number of charges in one element, \(Va\)
    • One Equivalent: \(OEq = N_A charges\);
    • \(\# Ions = \frac{\# Eq \times N_A}{Va}\)
    • Equivalent weight (weight per charge): \(W_e = \frac{W}{Va}\);
    • Weight: \(W = \frac{\# Eq}{Va}W_a\);
    • \(\#Eq = \frac{W \times Va}{W_a}\)
