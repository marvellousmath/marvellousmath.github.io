Landfill Components

  1. Base Liner System The liner system has as its main purpose to isolate the waste, preventing the contact of leachate with the ground water.A liner may consist of multiple barriers or a single one. The most used are compacted clay layer (CCL), geomembrane (GM) and geosynthetic clay layer (GCL).

  2. Side slope liners The side slope liners are very similar to the base liner, but they demand extra considerations such as slope stability.

  3. Final cover system The final cover system is designed to prevent the storm water to reach the waste, as well as to prevent the access of people and animals to the waste. Its design requires considerations such as water balance, settlements, erosion control, aesthetics among others. Different than the base liner system the final cover does not riquere consideration advection and difusion of chemical components.

  4. Liquid collection systems The role of the liquid collection system is to collect the leachate, preventing the build up of leachate head and limiting the amount of leakage that goes thought the liner

  5. Gas collection system Decomposition of the waste in a landfill generates an enormous amount of gas, which is collected by the gas collection system. Either the landfill gas is falred under controlled conditions or used in the production of energy.

  6. Ground Water monitoring systems The landfill operator need to maintain ground water monitoring systems during operation and after closure, to ensure that the amount of certain chemical susbtances and under a certain level that does not represent risk to human health or to the environment.

  7. Surface water management systems The surface water management systems controls the water in the surface in order the minimize erosion and infiltration of the water in the final cover system.

Base Lining System Configurations

The base liners consist in a barrier layer and a leachate collection system and they can be divided in four categories:

  • Single liners
  • Single composite liners
  • Double liners
  • Double composite liners

Single liners

A single liner consists only of one material in a single layer. That could be a compacted clay layer, a geoemembrane or a geosynthetic clay layer overlaid by the leachate collection system. A single liner can have significant amount of leakage even if the construction/installation is conducted with high standards. A single liner consisting of a compacted clay layer may have a significant amount of leakage due to permeation, even if the targeted hydraulic conductivity is achieved. A single layer of a geomembrane do not allow permeation if it is intact and the leakage due to diffusion through a geomembrane is insignificant.The problem is that it is impossible, practically speaking, to have the installation of the geomembrane without any whole or defects.

Single composite liners

Two different materials can be combined in order to form the single composite liner. Usually it consists in a geomembrane on top of a compacted clay layer or a geosynthetic clay layer, with the leachate collection system on top of the geomembrane. The geomembrane on top solves the problem of the permeation through the clay layer, and the clay layer underlying the geomembrane reduces siginifcantly the leakage rate through the geomembrane defects. Therefore, this combination results in minimum leakage.

Double liners

What differenciates the double liner is the presense of a second leatache colection system between two materials working as barriers. If it is a simple double liner, it usually consists of two geomembrane liners having one leatache collection system between them, which is called secondary collection system and another one at the top of the top geomembrane, which is called primary collection system. Such a system provides a extra barrier to permeation and it also enables the detection of lekage even before it start to penetrate the soil underneth the landfill.

Double composite liners

The combination of the concept of composite liners with the double liner results in the double composite liner. That is to say that it is composed of a primary and a secondary leachate collection system with a composite liner under the first system and another under the second system. This system has both the advantages of the composite liners and the double liners. That is, it reduces the leakage to a minimum rate and the provides the possibility of monitoring what this rate is.
