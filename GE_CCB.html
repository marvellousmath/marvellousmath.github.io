Compacted Clay Barriers

Compaction is the removal of air, which happens instantaneously. Consolidation is the removal of water and it is time dependent.

Purpose:

  • increase shear strength;
  • decrease hydraulic conductivity;
  • decrease liquefaction potential;
  • decrease compressibility or increase stiffness;
  • increase erosion resistance;
  • control volumeteric change.

Measurement of compaction

  • void ratio \(e=\frac{V_v}{V_s}\)
  • Dry unit weight \(\gamma _d = \frac{W_s}{V}\)

\(\gamma _d = \frac{W_s}{V}= \frac{G_s \gamma _w V_s}{V_s+V_v}= \frac{G_s \gamma _w V_s}{V_s+V_se} \to \gamma _d = \frac{G_s \gamma _w}{1+e}\)

Determining the dry unit weight

  1. measure γ;
  2. measure ω;
  3. compute γ/(1+ω).

Relative density

  • It is a relative measurement of density, using as a reference the maximum and minimum void ratio

  • \(e_{min}\): ASTM D4253 - vibratory table

  • \(e_{max}\): ASTM D4254 - three methods
    A. Use a funnel or a hand scoop to place material loosely in a mold;
    B. Deposit material in a mold by extracting a soil-filled tube;
    C. Deposit material by quickly inverting a 2000 ml graduated cylinder (soil is dry).

    \(D_r = \frac{e_{max}-e}{e_{max}-e_{min}}\times 100\)

Compaction energy

\(E_p = \frac{W\times h \times n_L \times n_b}{V}\)

Compaction curve

Zero air voids line (ZAV)

\(\gamma _d = \frac{G_s \gamma _w}{1+e}= \frac{G_s \gamma _w}{1+\frac{G_s\omega}{S}} = \frac{G_s S \gamma _w}{S+G_s \omega}\) \(S=1 \to \gamma _d = \frac{G_s \gamma _w}{1+G_s \omega}\)

Particle arrangement

  • Compaction energy: the particles can be forced to be more aligned to each other through the application of higher levels of compaction energy. Therefore, increasing the compaction energy leads to an increase in dispersion.
  • Water content: the DDL depends of on the avaliability of water to develop. Hence, higher water content lead to a thicker DDL, resulting in a increase of dispersion as well.

Properties of compacted clay

  1. Immediatelly after compaction (undrained loading, c and phi)
  2. Long after compaction (drained loading, c’ and phi’)
  3. Long after compaction (undrained loading, c and phi)
  1. Shear strength
    1. suction when compacted dry of optimum increases shear strength
    1. Effect of fabric is less significant; compaction water content has little effect on drained shear strength;
    1. For high confining stresses, basically the denser the soil, the greater the shear strength. If confining stress is low, compaction wet of optimum can even be beneficial.
  1. Stiffness (or compressibility)
  • The higher, the denser the soil. Stiffness may increase with time due to consolidation
  1. Hydraulic conductivity
    1. Suction when compacted dry of optimum may increase the initial hydraulic conductivity;
    1. Long term hydraulic conductivity depends more upon the fabric. Dispersed fabric, obtained when compacted wet of optimum, leads to a lower hydraulic conductivity;
    1. When the soil is wetted, the benefit of suction is lost and the hydraulic conductivity is the saturated hydraulic conductivity.

Design of clay barriers

  • 15 points method: 5 points for each energy of compaction
    1. Construct 3 compaction curves;
    2. Measure the desired properties for each specimen and plot the property as a function of water content;
    3. Construct contours.
  • Procedure by Daniel and Benson
    1. Construct 3 compaction curves;
    2. Measure the properties of interest. Determine the points that satisfy a given criteria;
    3. Define accptable zones
  • Type of compaction specifications
    1. End-Result
    • Specify
      • Relative compaction (RC);
      • (sometimes) water content (ω).
    • Contractor selects appropriate compaction equipment;
    • Perform quality control.
    1. Procedural
    • Begin with test fill
    • Specify
      • Water content (ω);
      • Type and weight of compaction equipment;
      • Number of passes;
      • Special requirements;
      • Maximum lift thickness.
    • Perform quality control.
  • Selection of compaction requirements
    1. Small projects
    • Often specify RC only;
    • See guidelines.
    1. Large projects
    • Specify both RC and ω;
    • Use agency guidelines/conduct limited testing.
    1. Critical projects
    • Specify both RC and ω;
    • Select requirements based on extensive testing;
    • Construct test fill at project site.
